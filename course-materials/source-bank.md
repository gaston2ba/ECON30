# Source Bank Starter

This file is a staging area for the documents, maps, and images you collect for the project. It is designed to help you keep sources organized before the final paper or presentation takes shape.

## Folder use

- `readings/`: PDFs, scanned chapters, and article downloads.
- `maps/`: historical maps, planning diagrams, and city plans.
- `images/`: photographs, postcards, screenshots, and visual reference material.

## First collection priorities

### Readings

- Oldenburg, Jacobs, and Gehl excerpts or PDFs if legally available through the library.
- Ward on Haussmann.
- Wakild on Porfirian Mexico City.
- Schenker for the comparative parks angle.

### Maps

- Haussmann-era plan of Paris showing boulevards and major park projects.
- Historical map of Mexico City from the Porfiriato.
- Map of `Paseo de la Reforma`, `Alameda Central`, and `Chapultepec Park`.
- Map or plan showing `Jardin des Tuileries`, `Bois de Boulogne` or `Parc des Buttes-Chaumont`, and a major boulevard district.

### Images

- Historic views or postcards of `Paseo de la Reforma`.
- Historic views of `Chapultepec Park` and `Alameda Central`.
- Historic views of `Jardin des Tuileries` and a Haussmann boulevard.
- Any visuals that show who is represented as using the space.

## Suggested naming pattern

Use filenames that stay readable later:

- `city_topic_author_or_source_year.pdf`
- `city_map_area_year.jpg`
- `city_site_view_year_source.jpg`

Examples:

- `paris_haussmann_ward_2015.pdf`
- `mexicocity_porfirian_map_1900.jpg`
- `paris_tuileries_postcard_1890.jpg`

## Source note template

When you add an item, note the following:

- `File name`
- `Source or database`
- `Date`
- `Why it matters`
- `Possible use in paper or map`

## Caution

Avoid downloading copyrighted materials from random sites. Prefer your university library, open-access repositories, museum collections, city archives, and clearly licensed image sources.
